My Skills.

Robotics & Control

I specialize in nonlinear adaptive control theory, system identification and robust control, with applications to robotics and high-performance aircraft. Prior to joining MIT AACL I was an Applied Mathematician at Aurora Flight Sciences, where I was the Principal Investigator for Aurora's DARPA AlphaDogfight team and led the development of autonomous combat algorithms for supersonic fighter jets

Software Development

I write code and develop software to actualize my research, projects and hobbies. Experienced in Python, MATLAB, C++ and JavaScript. Artificial Intelligence

My research focuses on the theory and development of novel, safe, and adaptable learning algorithms. Specifically, I am interested in the construction of reinforcement learning algorithms that engender trust and safety through implicit structure and proofs of robustness. These properties are necessary for the deployment of ML/AI solutions onto safety-critical systems
